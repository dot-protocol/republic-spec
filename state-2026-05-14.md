## WHAT IS A DOT

A DOT is zero bytes. It is the contact itself, existing in superposition until observed. Size is substrate-dependent and observation-dependent — what looks like 153 bytes in a V1 digital encoding is the postcard about the contact, not the contact. What looks like 800 bytes of brotli storefront seeded into a V23 QR code is the cell, not the byte count. The cell has inside and outside, functions, growth, decay. It is the unit of computation post-binary — not the bit.

The DOT is the answer to the twelve-dependency wound: every human in 2026 rents the right to be present in the world from intermediaries who can revoke it. Phone numbers from carriers, addresses from DNS, websites from hosting providers, storefronts from platforms, identity from states, money from banks, audience from social platforms, software from app stores, compute from cloud providers, truth-verification from journalists, two more. Twelve revocable, metered, surveilled, deplatformable dependencies. The DOT replaces every one of them with a physical, cryptographic, content-addressed, capability-gated artifact the holder carries. Sticker. Tattoo. Printed cell. Coin. Stone.

**R35 extension:** the DOT is also a register in a gauge-theoretic computer (the Republic, recognized as one), an index for observer-relocation under dust theory, and a participant in a reversible-computing thermal-gradient-rider. These are not three new things — they are the same DOT viewed from three new angles. The DOT did not grow. The reading grew.
